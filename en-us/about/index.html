About

I'm Anderson Anzileiro (anzi), a software engineer who likes building systems from the metal up and training small language models. I've spent over a decade shipping software, including time at Nubank and as a Staff Engineer at Stone, and founding a startup along the way.

These days most of my energy goes into Maracatu, an open-source initiative from Brazil working on foundation models, civic AI and network infrastructure. I train open-weight Brazilian Portuguese LLMs from scratch, write low-level systems in Rust, and build tools that put public data within everyone's reach.

This blog is where I keep articles, notes and write-ups about that work, mostly so future me can find them again, and maybe they help you too. Everything here is open source.

What I'm building

  • Maracatu

    Open-weight Brazilian Portuguese LLMs, trained from scratch.

  • Calunga

  • catraca
